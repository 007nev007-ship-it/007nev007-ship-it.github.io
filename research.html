The Research Behind BridgeEDU

BridgeEDU is grounded in a Master’s-level research study examining communication between school leaders and foreign English language teachers in Thai educational settings.

The research combines a meta-analysis of existing studies with real-world teacher data to provide both theoretical insight and practical application.

Key Finding

ICT (Information and Communication Technology) can significantly improve communication in intercultural school environments — but only when it is used with clarity and structure.

Meta-Analysis Overview

A systematic review of four studies was conducted to examine the relationship between ICT use and communication effectiveness in educational contexts.

  • Strong overall positive relationship identified
  • High variability across different contexts
  • Results highlight the importance of implementation, not just access

Teacher Survey Insights

A targeted survey of foreign English language teachers provided additional insight into everyday communication experiences within schools.

  • Digital tools are widely used but inconsistently applied
  • Clarity of message is more important than tool selection
  • Miscommunication often stems from structure, not language ability

What This Means in Practice

The research does not suggest that more technology is the solution. Instead, it shows that structured communication practices are essential.

  • Simple, clear messaging improves outcomes
  • Consistency reduces confusion
  • Shared expectations strengthen communication across cultures
